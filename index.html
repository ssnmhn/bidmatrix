How it works

Five days, five steps, two of them yours.

1

You send the bid · you, ~20 min

The RFP (with all addenda), your past proposals or capability docs, and a structured intake where you certify the facts of your business — years, licenses, references, staff. We work only from what you certify.

2

Matrix lands same day

The compliance matrix arrives first, fast — because it's also a bid/no-bid instrument. If there's an eligibility gate you can't clear, you find out before you spend a week writing, not after the tabulation posts.

3

Outline, then draft

Structure mirrors the RFP's own order; depth follows the points. The draft echoes the evaluation criteria's language, answer-first, in your firm's register.

4

Red-team pass

Three simulated evaluators score the draft against the published criteria. You get the deduction ledger and the top fixes ranked by points-per-hour of your time.

5

You confirm flags, sign, submit · you, ~1–2 hrs

Resolve the highlighted flags, run the disqualification checklist with us, and submit through your portal. We never submit on your behalf — your name, your bid, your relationship with the agency.

FAQ

Is it allowed to get help writing a government proposal?

Yes — proposal consultants are an established industry serving government contractors at every size; agencies evaluate the proposal, and the facts and signature are yours. The rare solicitation that restricts or requires disclosure of preparation help gets caught in our matrix pass, and we tell you exactly what it says.

How can you be 5–10× cheaper than a proposal consultant?

Because frontier AI does the reading and drafting, and it is genuinely better at 100-page rule-extraction than a tired human at hour nine. A human reviews before anything ships. You're not paying for our office; you're paying for a disciplined system.

What if my bid isn't winnable?

Then the matrix says so, same day, and you've spent $0 — the eligibility check comes before payment. We'd rather tell you "don't bid this one, here's the gate you can't clear" than take $500 to decorate a dead proposal.

Do you guarantee a win?

No, and be suspicious of anyone who does — evaluation panels are human and competition is real. What we target is the failure class you control completely: disqualification, missed forms, buried answers, and points lost to structure rather than substance.

Who sees my documents?

The engagement team and the AI systems doing the work — nobody else, nothing public, deleted on request. Client materials never appear in samples or marketing. (The demo package on this site was built entirely from public records of a closed solicitation.)
